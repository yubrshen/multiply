What is "upper bound" of a number?

For a numbe x, any nmuber y greater than x, then y is a upper bound of x.

For example, 7 is greater than 5, 7 is a upper bound of 5.

What is "lower bound" of a number?

For a numbe x, any nmuber y smaller than x, then y is a lower bound of x.

For example, 3 is greater than 5, 3 is a lower bound of 5.

 

If you don't know the exact answer to a problem, often exploring the upper/lower bounds would be helpful in practice, and in getting the eventual answer.
